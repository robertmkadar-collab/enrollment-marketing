Now, the hard part

How to build the machine that generates the dashboard — that's the real magic.

Anyone can build a pretty chart from a CSV. The real work — the part that turns "we ran some ads" into "here's exactly what an enrolled student in our MBA program cost us, after multi-touch attribution and a 9-month consideration window" — is wiring the systems together. That's where most enrollment marketing teams get stuck. It's also what I've done at every job for the last 20 years.

01 / Source systems

Your data lives in too many places

Lead capture in one CRM, applications in another, ad spend across five platforms, enrollments in your SIS. Joining them is not "an API call." It's deduping a person across UTMs, emails, form submissions, and a 9-month consideration window.

02 / Ad platforms

Spend ≠ Cost-per-Enrolled-Student

Platform-reported conversions are the start, not the answer. Real attribution means stitching click IDs to leads, leads to apps, apps to admits, admits to deposits — and reconciling when a "Meta-attributed" lead actually came from organic search.
